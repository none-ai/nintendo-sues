Timeline of Events

Chronological development of the Nintendo tariff lawsuit

March 2026

Lawsuit Filed

Nintendo officially files a lawsuit against the US government seeking a refund on tariffs. The company claims misclassification of products led to overpayment.

January 2026

Final Tariff Payment

Nintendo makes its final tariff payment under protest while preparing legal action.

December 2025

Administrative Appeal Denied

The US Customs and Border Protection denies Nintendo's administrative appeal challenging the tariff classification.

August 2025

Appeal Submitted

Nintendo submits a formal administrative appeal to challenge the tariff classification.

March 2025

Audit Results Released

US Customs completes its audit of Nintendo's imports, confirming the higher tariff classification.

September 2024

Customs Audit Begins

US Customs and Border Protection initiates an audit of Nintendo's import records going back to 2019.

January 2019

New Tariffs Implemented

Additional tariffs on electronics go into effect as part of ongoing trade negotiations.
